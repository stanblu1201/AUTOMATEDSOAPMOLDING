Automated soap molding machines typically consist of a conveyor system, molds for shaping the soap, a mechanism for dispensing liquid soap into the molds and curing the soap, and controls for adjusting settings such as temperature and mold shape. They may also include limit switch for monitoring production and ensuring quality, when the limit switch hit, the molder detects and start the filling process.
Perform repairs with DRY hands and in DRY condition. Perform minor repairs with caution. Contact our team if major repairs must be done. Never use the machine which has been damaged or with a defective wires. Turn off the power first before touching any components and wires inside the Control Box.
  • Ensuring that moving parts are adequately enclosed or protected to prevent contact with operators.
  • Installing emergency stop buttons to quickly shut down the machine in case of emergencies.
  • Implementing systems that prevent the machine from operating if safety guards are not properly closed or if there is a fault detected.
  • Providing thorough training for operators on safe operating procedures and potential hazards associated with the machine.  Conducting routine maintenance checks to ensure the machine is in good working condition and addressing any issues promptly.
  • Keeping detailed documentation of safety procedures, maintenance records, and any incidents or near misses for future reference and improvement.

1. Power On:

· Plug the power cable to the electrical outlet

· Turn ON the circuit breaker

· Confirm that the red-light indicator is illuminated, indicating that the machine is powered.

 

2. Filling the Machine:

· Pour the liquid in the hopper.

· Press the Start Button (Green) on the machine interface.

 

3. Power Off:

· After use, press the Stop Button (Red) and turn off the circuit breaker to turn off the machine.
